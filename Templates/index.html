About the Sharpe Ratio

The Sharpe ratio measures the performance of an investment compared to a risk-free asset, after adjusting for its risk. It's calculated as the difference between the returns of the investment and the risk-free return, divided by the standard deviation of the investment returns.

A higher Sharpe ratio indicates better risk-adjusted performance.
